Trader Review: December 24, 2024

Overview of the Trade

  • Stock: TSLA
  • Position: Bearish put options
  • Thesis: Expected TSLA to reverse downward after a strong run, based on technical resistance, volume divergence, and overbought RSI conditions.
  • Execution: Entered on potential rejection near $438 with an initial stop-loss planned above $440. Held the position through further price increases, anticipating a drop by Friday.

Key Events

  1. Setup Identified:

    The initial setup aligned with a potential A+ bearish play. Overbought RSI and proximity to resistance suggested a short-term pullback.

  2. Challenges During the Trade:

    TSLA broke through resistance, hitting $450+ with strong momentum. Despite invalidation of the original thesis, the position was held in hopes of a longer-term reversal by Friday.

  3. Outcome:

    TSLA reached $465, extending gains and invalidating the bearish thesis. Losses were larger than anticipated due to holding past the predefined exit point.

What Went Well

  • Solid Technical Analysis: The initial identification of resistance and overbought conditions was valid. Correlation with broader market sentiment was considered.
  • Willingness to Take Risk: Confidence in the thesis showed a willingness to make decisive moves.

What Went Wrong

  • Failure to Exit on Invalid Thesis: The trade plan was clear, but execution faltered when resistance was broken. The position was held based on emotion and hope rather than updated technical signals.
  • Overexposure: Allocating 50% of available funds ($400 out of $800) left little room for adjustments or recovery trades.
  • Ignoring Key Signs: Low trading volume and bullish weekly trends were signs the market wasn’t aligned with the bearish thesis.

Lessons Learned

  • Respect Your Stop-Loss: A stop-loss is there for a reason. Exiting at $440 would have minimized losses significantly.
  • Stay Flexible: When the thesis is invalidated, adapt. Either exit the trade or reassess for another entry.
  • Risk Management for Small Accounts: Allocating a smaller percentage of the account (e.g., 20-30%) provides room for recovery and reduces emotional pressure.
  • Weekly Trends Matter: Higher timeframes carry more weight. TSLA’s strong weekly chart suggested further upside, which conflicted with the bearish daily setup.

Next Steps

  • Reevaluate the Trading Plan: Incorporate stricter stop-loss adherence and focus on aligning setups with broader trends.
  • Implement Scaling: Start with smaller positions and scale in as the trade works in your favor.
  • Limit Emotional Holding: Use conditional orders to exit trades automatically when invalidation levels are breached.
  • Set Clearer Exit Plans: Define not just stop-loss levels but also when to take partial profits or exit entirely if conditions shift.

Final Thoughts

This trade serves as a reminder of the importance of discipline and adaptability. While the initial analysis was solid, failing to act on invalidation turned a manageable loss into a significant setback. Moving forward, the focus will be on tighter risk management and sticking to predefined plans. Every trade, win or lose, is a chance to grow as a trader.
