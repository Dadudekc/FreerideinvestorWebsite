Review Trade Metrics for Better Performance

Metrics don’t just tell you if a trade was profitable…they reveal the patterns that make or break your strategy. Reviewing them isn’t about obsessing over numbers…it’s about learning what works, what doesn’t, and how to fine-tune your approach. Let’s break it down.

Why Metrics Matter

You can’t fix what you don’t measure. Tracking metrics gives you…

  • A no-BS look at your strengths and weaknesses.
  • Clarity on whether your strategy is actually working over time.
  • A way to make decisions based on facts, not emotions.

If you want consistent results, metrics aren’t optional…they’re the foundation of the game.

Key Metrics to Track

Focus on what actually matters…no fluff, just actionable insights:

1. Profit Percentage

This tells you how much you made relative to your investment.

Formula:

Profit Percentage = ((Exit Price - Entry Price) / Entry Price) × 100

Example: Bought at $0.75, sold at $0.84. Your profit percentage is:

((0.84 - 0.75) / 0.75) × 100 = 12%

2. Risk/Reward Ratio

How much you stood to gain versus how much you risked.

Formula:

Risk/Reward Ratio = Potential Profit / Potential Loss

Example: Target was $0.84, stop-loss was $0.70. Your ratio is:

(0.84 - 0.75) / (0.75 - 0.70) = 1.8

3. Win Rate

The percentage of your trades that were profitable.

Formula:

Win Rate = (Winning Trades / Total Trades) × 100

Example: 8 wins out of 10 trades gives you:

(8 / 10) × 100 = 80%

4. Holding Time

Are you holding too long or bailing too early? Check how long you’re staying in trades and compare winners vs. losers.

How to Track Metrics

You don’t need anything fancy…just keep it simple:

  • Log your entry and exit prices.
  • Write down your stop-loss and target levels.
  • Note the outcome: profit, loss, or break-even.
  • Add how long you held the trade.

Over time, you’ll start spotting patterns…like leaving profits on the table or holding losing trades too long.

Tools to Make It Easier

If manual tracking feels like a drag, here are some ideas:

  • Trading Platforms: Most platforms, like Thinkorswim or TradingView, let you review trade history and basic stats.
  • Spreadsheets: Google Sheets or Excel with a few simple formulas can do the job.
  • Future Widgets: Imagine plugging in your trade details and instantly seeing your win rate or risk/reward ratio. (This is something we’ll build later.)

What to Do with the Insights

The point of tracking metrics isn’t to feel good (or bad) about your trades…it’s to make better decisions. Once you’ve reviewed your numbers:

  • Lean Into Your Strengths: Double down on setups that consistently deliver.
  • Fix the Weak Spots: If your losses come from holding too long, tighten up your stop-losses. If you’re exiting too soon, work on scaling out of trades.
  • Measure Progress: Track your metrics regularly to see if you’re improving…and if not, adjust.

Final Thoughts

Metrics don’t lie…but they only work if you use them. Start tracking today, and let the numbers guide you to smarter trades.
