My Trading Journal: Tesla Calls

How I capitalized on Tesla’s volatility and my outlook for the upcoming rally.

Overview

After catching the dip from Tesla last night with puts, I reinvested those profits into calls.
With my bullish outlook heading into the weekend, I’m expecting Tesla to rally sharply, especially
as FOMO kicks in ahead of Monday’s session.

Table of Contents




Trade Details

What I Did

  • Closed puts for profit after Tesla dipped as expected.
  • Rolled profits into overnight calls (strikes: $417.5, $437.5, $445).

Why I Did It

  • Anticipating FOMO-driven buying ahead of Monday’s rally to $450+.
  • Strong support at $414 and improving momentum indicators (MACD, RSI).
  • Expected Tesla’s volatility to amplify gains.

Market Insights

What I’m Seeing Now

– Tesla closed above $415, showing signs of strength.
– Order book reveals strong buy support at $414 and moderate resistance at $417.
– Overnight trading suggests positive sentiment for tomorrow’s open.

Key Risks

  • Overnight trading sentiment might not carry into the open.
  • Failure to break above $417 could stall the rally.
  • Time decay on short-dated options will accelerate tomorrow.

Lessons and Adjustments

What I’ll Do Differently Next Time

  • Consider closer-to-the-money strikes for short-term plays.
  • Allocate only part of the profits for overnight plays to reduce risk.
  • Wait for intraday confirmation before rolling into new positions.

Action Plan for Tomorrow

  1. Monitor pre-market for strength above $415 and volume trends.
  2. Exit calls if Tesla struggles to hold $414 at the open.
  3. Take profits quickly if Tesla rallies past $417 toward $420.
