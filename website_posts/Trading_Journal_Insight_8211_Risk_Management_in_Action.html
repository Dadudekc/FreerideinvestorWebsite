Today’s Trade Recap

Today, I stuck to my risk management plan, and while it resulted in a loss, it was a controlled one. TSLA broke my key support at 393, confirming the bearish trend I had been watching. Instead of trying to fight the market, I made the disciplined choice to cut the trade before it spiraled.

How to Fight the Urge to Overwatch the Chart

One of the toughest parts of trading is resisting the urge to constantly monitor the chart, especially after taking a loss. But true discipline means sticking to the plan and not reacting emotionally.

  • Set It and Walk Away
    • Define your entry, stop-loss, and target before executing.
    • Use alerts to notify you when key levels hit.
    • Close the tab or app—out of sight, out of mind.
  • Stick to a Review Schedule
    • Only check at planned intervals (e.g., every 30 minutes).
    • Use structured breaks to reset and avoid emotional reactions.

Lessons from Today

Key Takeaway: Sticking to the plan matters more than any single trade. Seeing TSLA move higher after my exit could have triggered FOMO, but I knew my setup was invalidated. There’s no regret in following proper risk management.

  • Discipline over Emotion: A loss within the plan is better than a reckless hold.
  • Adjust but Don’t Overreact: If the market trend shifts, adapt for the next setup.
  • Patience Wins: The right trade will come—chasing rarely pays off.

Final Thoughts

Losses happen. What matters is how we handle them. Today was a test of discipline, and I passed. Tomorrow, I come back with the same focus: smart setups, defined risk, and no emotional trades.

Trade with intention, not reaction.
