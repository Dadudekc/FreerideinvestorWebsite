Trading Journal Insight: The Power of Precision

Date: January 3, 2025

Trade: TSLA $400 Call

Result: +12% Gain in 26 Minutes

The Setup

At 8:32 AM, I entered the TSLA $400 call for $0.75. The chart looked primed for a move:

  • RSI: 54.63—neutral but leaning bullish, giving me room for upward momentum.
  • Bollinger Bands: Price hugging the lower band, a classic signal for potential reversal.
  • MACD: Hinted at bullish momentum building, with the lines starting to cross upwards.

Volume was steady, and the VWAP was aligned, giving me confidence that I wasn’t stepping into choppy waters.

The Execution

The decision to enter felt good…calculated, not rushed. What I’m particularly proud of was the exit.

At 8:58 AM, I sold at $0.84. Here’s why that moment stood out:

  • RSI: 72.82—moving into overbought territory, often a warning sign of a potential pullback.
  • Bollinger Bands: Price touched the upper band, confirming the upward move might be running out of steam.
  • MACD: Still bullish, but peaking, signaling a slowdown might be near.

The key here was recognizing that I didn’t need to capture every last cent. The goal was to ride the momentum wave and step off before the market turned. And I did exactly that.

What I Learned

  • Timing Is Everything: Entering when multiple indicators align (RSI, Bollinger Bands, MACD) gives me an edge. But just as important was sticking to the plan and not getting greedy on the exit.
  • Momentum Works…Until It Doesn’t: The sell decision was reinforced by the overbought RSI and Bollinger Band signals. Waiting any longer could have exposed me to unnecessary risk.
  • The Stop Market Sell Is a Lifesaver: By setting a stop trigger, I locked in gains while leaving no room for hesitation. This tool isn’t just about risk management…it’s about emotional discipline.

Opportunities to Grow

  • Scaling the Position: One contract is fine for learning, but what if I’d entered with 3 contracts and scaled out at different levels? Something to try next time.
  • Trend Confirmation: Checking higher time frames (5-minute or 15-minute) might have added more conviction to my entry.
  • Post-Exit Analysis: Was there more upside after my exit? If so, what signals could have told me to stay in longer?

Final Thought

This trade was a win—not just because of the profit but because of how well it aligned with my strategy. It’s proof that sticking to the technicals and trusting my tools leads to consistent results.

The market doesn’t reward hesitation or greed…it rewards preparation, discipline, and the ability to let go at the right time.

What I’ll take forward: Be precise, be disciplined, and always respect the indicators.
