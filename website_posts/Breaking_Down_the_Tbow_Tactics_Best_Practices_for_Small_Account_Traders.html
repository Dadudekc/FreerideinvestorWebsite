The Tbow Tactics focus on momentum-based entries using MACD crossovers, VWAP, and EMAs while keeping the core principle of mean reversion intact. This strategy works great for both small and large accounts, but when trading with a small account, execution, risk management, and discipline are key.

Why This Style Works

  • Momentum Confirmation: MACD crossovers provide clear entry signals when combined with VWAP and EMA alignment.
  • Structured Exits: Targeting moving averages (50 or 200 MA) ensures you’re locking in profits at logical points.
  • Small Account Friendly: Tight stops and predefined risk-reward ratios help protect your capital while letting your trades run.

Best Practices for This Trading Style

1. Respect VWAP

VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is your guide for sentiment:

  • Above VWAP: The market is bullish. Look for long setups.
  • Below VWAP: The market is bearish. Focus on shorts.

Tip: Never go against VWAP unless you see strong confirmation from other indicators.

2. Master the MACD

The MACD crossover is your signal to act:

  • Bullish Crossover: MACD line crosses above the Signal line → Momentum shift up.
  • Bearish Crossover: MACD line crosses below the Signal line → Momentum shift down.
  • Volume Matters: Avoid false signals during low-volume periods (e.g., midday chop).

Tip: Use MACD in combination with VWAP and EMA alignment for high-probability trades.

3. Trade with the EMAs

The EMA (Exponential Moving Average) helps define trend strength:

  • 8 > 21 EMA: Bullish sentiment. Use for long entries.
  • 8 < 21 EMA: Bearish sentiment. Look for short setups.

Tip: Use EMAs as dynamic support/resistance levels for trailing stops or re-entry points.

4. Focus on Mean Reversion

At the heart of this strategy is mean reversion. Price tends to return to the mean (moving averages) after sharp moves:

  • Primary Target: Closest moving average (50 or 200 MA).
  • Secondary Target: Bollinger Band extremes if price overextends before hitting the MA.

Tip: Don’t get greedy. Scale out profits near logical levels like the moving averages or Bollinger Bands.

Small Account Tips

1. Manage Risk Aggressively

With a small account, you can’t afford large drawdowns:

  • Risk no more than 10% per trade (as per the Tbow mindset).
  • Set tight stop-losses just beyond VWAP or the nearest EMA.
  • Never chase a trade that doesn’t align with your setup.

2. Trade High-Probability Setups

Only trade when all conditions align:

  • MACD crossover confirmed.
  • VWAP alignment (price above/below based on direction).
  • EMA trend supporting the move.
  • Volume backing the price action.

Tip: Sit on the sidelines if the setup isn’t clear. Missing a trade is better than taking a bad one.

3. Be Disciplined with Exits

Exits are critical for small accounts:

  • Scale out profits near Bollinger Bands or partial moving averages.
  • Exit fully at the moving average or if momentum shifts against you.
  • Close trades immediately if price crosses VWAP in the wrong direction.

Tip: Always plan your exit before entering a trade. The market owes you nothing—lock in profits!

Why This Works for Small Accounts

This style of trading minimizes risk while maximizing opportunities. By sticking to tight stops, momentum entries, and mean-reversion exits, you can grow a small account quickly without exposing yourself to unnecessary losses. It’s about precision, discipline, and letting the math play out over time.
